about: A world of precious wearable objects.

FOTU is a wearable art studio founded by Welsh artist Abigail Hodges.

Each piece begins as an emotion, usually an uncomfortable one, and ends as something you can put on your body. The materials are recycled. The prints are handmade. The objects are one of a kind.

It started with an uncanny feeling. The kind that lives just below language. People around her were exhausted and medicated. Nature had been replaced by concrete and screens. The news ran on a loop: war, burning, extinction. It felt like a puzzle where the missing piece wasn't information, it was something else entirely. The 3am wake-ups confirmed it. Climate anxiety, the doctors call it.

What it actually was, was a signal. The answer wasn't to think harder. It was to make something. From the darkest feelings came the most vivid ideas. Then prints. Then dresses. What the old system called waste, discarded materials, difficult emotions, climate grief too big to name, FOTU treats as primary material. Plastic bottles become fabric. Fear becomes print. There is no waste. Only matter and feeling waiting for its next form.

Pieces come in two forms. Powermesh and lycra spun from recycled plastic bottles, printed with a waterless process, vivid, form-fitting, built to move. Or upcycled garments, hand painted and hand printed, each one singular. Dresses, tops, objects, jewellery. The prints trace patterns found in slime moulds, river tributaries, the veins of an eye. The same geometry, repeating everywhere. A reminder that it's all one thing. Influenced by London's underground music scene. Worn by people who dance for hours and think about the universe on the way home.

When you buy a FOTU piece, you're not buying a product. You're buying into a system. Dark feelings become beautiful objects. Waste becomes something precious. Everything is connected, and the fabric holding it together is love. FOTU. The studio is always open.
