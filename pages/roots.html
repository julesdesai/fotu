The Roots of FOTU

A world of precious wearable objects.

Dark feelings become beautiful objects. Waste becomes something precious. Everything is connected, and the fabric holding it together is love.

A wearable art studio founded by Welsh artist Abigail Hodges.

Each piece begins as an emotion, usually an uncomfortable one, and ends as something you can put on your body. The materials are recycled. The prints are handmade.

It started underground. With an uncanny feeling, the kind that lives just below language. People around felt exhausted and medicated. Nature seems replaced by concrete and screens. The news runs on a loop: war, burning, extinction. It feels like a puzzle where the missing piece isn't information, but something else entirely. The 3am wake-ups confirmed it. I needed to do something.

The feelings are signals. What you won't look at underground won't let you grow above it. And from the darkest feelings came the most vivid ideas. For me, prints. Then dresses.

"Emotion, which is suffering, ceases to be suffering as soon as we form a clear and distinct picture of it." - Spinoza

What the old system called waste, discarded materials, difficult emotions, climate grief too big to name, FOTU treats as primary material. Plastic bottles become fabric. Fear is clarified through symbols. There is no waste, only matter and feeling waiting for its next form.

Pieces come in two forms. Powermesh and lycra spun from recycled plastic bottles, printed with a waterless process, vivid, form-fitting, built to move. Or upcycled garments, hand painted and hand printed, each one singular. Dresses, tops, objects, jewellery. The mesh itself is a kind of argument: threads crossing threads, everything touching everything else.

The prints trace patterns found in slime moulds, river tributaries, the veins of an eye. Roots and branches. The same geometry repeating at every scale, which is not metaphor, it is just how matter organises itself. Emotions included. A reflection of the connectedness of all things, and an equality that exists in the world once we drop the anthropomorphic lens.

Influenced by London's underground music scene. Worn by people who dance for hours and think about the universe on the way home.
